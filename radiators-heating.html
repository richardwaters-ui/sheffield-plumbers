Common Questions

Got questions? Here's the quick answers.

How much does it cost to fix a radiator in Sheffield?
Radiator repairs in Sheffield typically cost £80-£150. Bleeding a radiator or replacing a valve costs £60-£100. Replacing a leaking radiator costs £150-£250 including a new rad. A full system power flush costs £400-£600 depending on the number of radiators. We'll diagnose the issue and give you a fixed price before starting work.
How long does a radiator repair take?
Bleeding radiators takes 10-15 minutes per rad. Replacing a radiator valve takes 45 minutes to an hour once we've drained down the system. Fitting a new radiator takes within 2 hours. A full power flush takes 4-6 hours depending on how many radiators you've got. We'll give you a time estimate once we've assessed the job, whether you're in City Centre, Hillsborough, or Ecclesall.
Can I bleed a radiator myself?
Yes, it's easy. You need a radiator key (£1 from hardware shops). Turn off the heating, insert the key into the bleed valve at the top corner of the radiator, turn anti-clockwise a quarter turn, and let the air hiss out. When water starts dribbling, close it. Have a cloth ready to catch drips. If you need to bleed radiators every few weeks, you've got a bigger issue, call us.
Why is one radiator cold when the others are hot?
If it's cold at the top and warm at the bottom, it needs bleeding (air trapped inside). If it's cold all over, the valve is stuck closed or sludge is blocking the rad. Try opening the valve fully. If that doesn't work, it needs flushing or replacing. A single cold radiator usually means a localised blockage, not a whole system problem.
What's a power flush and do I need one?
A power flush forces water and cleaning chemicals through your heating system at high pressure to remove sludge and rust. You need one if your radiators are cold at the bottom, your boiler is noisy, the water in the system is black or brown, or your heating is slow to warm up. It costs £400-£600 and extends your boiler's life by years.
Can you replace a radiator without draining the whole system?
Yes, if your radiators have isolating valves on both sides. We close the valves, remove the old rad, and fit the new one without draining the entire system. If there are no valves (common in older properties), we'll need to drain down or fit valves first. Either way, we'll get it done the same day.
Why is my radiator leaking from the valve?
Valve leaks are usually caused by a worn spindle seal or loose gland nut. Sometimes tightening the nut stops the leak. If not, we'll replace the valve or repack the gland. It's a straightforward job costing £80-£120. Don't ignore it, even a small leak can cause damp damage and rust to floorboards over time.
Can you add an extra radiator to my heating system?
Yes, if your boiler has enough capacity for another radiator. We'll assess your system, run new pipework, fit the radiator, and balance the system so all rads heat evenly. Adding a single radiator costs £300-£500 depending on pipe run length and rad size. If your boiler is undersized, we'll advise on upgrading it first.
