Common Questions

Got questions? Here's the quick answers.

How much does it cost to fix a dripping tap in Sheffield?
Fixing a dripping tap in Sheffield typically costs £60-£100. If it just needs a new washer, that's £60-£80. If the whole cartridge or valve needs replacing, expect £80-£120. Modern mixer taps sometimes need the entire tap replacing if internal parts are worn, which costs £150-£250 including a mid-range tap and fitting.
How long does a tap repair take?
Most tap repairs take 30-60 minutes. A simple washer replacement is done in 20 minutes. Replacing a cartridge or fitting a new tap takes 45 minutes to an hour. If the pipework underneath is old and needs updating, allow up to 2 hours, whether you're in City Centre, Hillsborough, or Ecclesall.
Can I fix a dripping tap myself?
If it's a traditional tap with a rubber washer, yes, turn off the water, unscrew the tap head, replace the washer (10p from a hardware shop), reassemble. For modern mixer taps with ceramic cartridges, it's trickier, you need the right cartridge for your tap model, and they're easy to fit incorrectly. If you're not confident, call us.
Why does my toilet keep running after I flush it?
A constantly running toilet usually means a worn flapper valve or siphon seal in the cistern. Water leaks from the cistern into the bowl instead of stopping when the cistern refills. This can waste 200+ litres per day and push your water bill up. Replacing the flapper or siphon costs £70-£100 and takes about an hour.
Can you fix a toilet that won't flush properly?
Yes. Weak flushing is usually caused by a faulty siphon, low water level in the cistern, or limescale blocking the rim jets. We'll diagnose the issue and fix it, that might mean replacing the siphon (£80-£120), adjusting the fill valve, or descaling the pan. Most jobs are sorted within an hour.
What causes a tap to judder when I turn it on?
Juddering or hammering taps are caused by high water pressure or loose pipework. When you turn the tap off quickly, the sudden stop sends a shockwave through the pipes (called water hammer). We can fit a pressure-reducing valve, secure loose pipes, or install arrestors to stop the hammering. Cost is £100-£180 depending on the fix needed.
Is a leaking toilet base an emergency?
Not immediately, but don't ignore it. A leak at the base usually means the wax seal between the toilet and the waste pipe has failed. If left, it can rot your floorboards and cause a smell. We'll lift the toilet, replace the seal, and reseat it properly. The job costs £100-£150 and takes within 2 hours.
Can you replace just the toilet seat or do I need a new toilet?
Toilet seats are easy to replace, you can do it yourself with a screwdriver in 10 minutes. Buy a universal-fit seat from any DIY shop for £15-£40. We only replace the whole toilet if the pan is cracked, the flush mechanism can't be repaired, or you want to upgrade to a modern low-flush model. New toilet supply and fit costs £200-£350.
