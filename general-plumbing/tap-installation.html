New taps? We'll fit them properly.

Professional tap installation across Sheffield.

New taps transform a kitchen or bathroom, but they need fitting correctly. The right connections, proper sealing, secure fixing, and no leaks. DIY installations often leak from the base, drip from connections underneath, or the taps work loose because they're not secured properly. In Sheffield's older properties around Crookes and Sneinton, non-standard pipe positions make tap fitting trickier than it looks.

We fit all tap types: kitchen mixer taps, bathroom basin taps, bath taps, and wall-mounted taps. We connect to existing pipework, fit new isolation valves if needed, seal properly, and test under pressure. Most installations take 45 minutes to 1 hour.

We operate across all Sheffield areas. No call-out fee. All tap installations carry a 12-month guarantee.

Types of Taps We Install

Kitchen mixer taps

Single-lever or twin-handle mixers. Deck-mounted (through sink) or wall-mounted. Pull-out spray heads for washing up. We fit them all.

Bathroom basin taps

Pillar taps (separate hot and cold), basin mixers, or mono-bloc single-hole mixers. We match tap centres to your basin holes.

Bath taps

Deck-mounted on the bath rim, wall-mounted for freestanding baths, or bath/shower mixers with diverter. We ensure correct hot and cold positioning.

Wall-mounted taps

For basins without tap holes or freestanding baths. Pipe connections are concealed in the wall. Trickier to install but look very clean.

What's Included in Tap Installation

Removal of old taps

We disconnect and remove old taps carefully. If fittings are seized, we cut them free without damaging the sink or basin.

New isolation valves

We fit isolation valves on hot and cold supplies if there aren't any. This lets you turn off water to the tap for future maintenance without draining the system.

Secure fixing

We mount taps properly with backnut and washers, seal with plumber's putty or silicone, and tighten securely so they don't work loose.

Connect and test

We connect flexible hoses to supplies (or rigid copper for wall-mounted taps), turn on water, check for leaks, and test hot/cold operation and flow.

Common Tap Installation Issues

DIY installations often have these problems:

Leaking from the base

No sealant under the tap base or the fixing isn't tight. Water seeps underneath and damages the worktop. We seal properly.

Loose taps

The backnut isn't tightened or there's no retaining plate on thin sinks. Taps move when you use them. We secure properly with top-hats or backing plates.

Wrong tap centres

The tap holes don't match the tap centres. Pillar taps on basins designed for mono-bloc. We check compatibility before ordering.

Undersized flexible hoses

Cheap hoses restrict flow. Kitchen taps especially need good flow. We use quality hoses with correct bore size.
