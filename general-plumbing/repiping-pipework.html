If your pipes are original to a Victorian or Edwardian property, they're probably lead, iron, or very old copper that's corroded from the inside. These fail regularly, cause low pressure, contaminate water, and cost you money in repeated leak repairs. Re-piping means replacing the old pipework with modern plastic (PEX or Hep2O) or new copper. It's the permanent fix that stops the cycle of leaks and repairs Common across Sheffield, from Hillsborough to City Centre.

We re-pipe sections or entire properties depending on what's failing. We can replace just the problem area, or if multiple sections are showing age, we'll quote for replacing all the supply pipes in one job. Modern plastic pipe lasts 50+ years, doesn't corrode, and is faster to install than copper. Most re-piping projects take 1-3 days depending on scope.

We operate across all Sheffield areas. No call-out fee. All pipework carries a 12-month guarantee.

Signs You Need Re-piping

⚠️

Repeated leaks

If you're repairing leaks every few months, the pipes are at the end of their life. Patching one section doesn't help when the rest is corroding too.

⚠️

Low water pressure everywhere

Corroded pipes narrow from inside, restricting flow. If all taps are weak and cleaning aerators doesn't help, the pipes need replacing.

⚠️

Discoloured water

Brown or rusty water from internal pipe corrosion. This is common in old iron or galvanised steel pipes. The water is unsafe and the pipes need replacing.

⚠️

Visible corrosion

Green stains on copper pipes, rust on iron pipes, or grey dull lead pipes. Once corrosion is visible externally, it's far worse internally.

⚠️

Pipes over 50 years old

If your property still has original pipework from the 1960s or earlier, it's overdue for replacement. Even if it hasn't failed yet, it will.

Copper vs Plastic Pipe

Modern re-piping uses either copper or plastic. Both have pros and cons:

Copper pipe (traditional)

Durable, heat-resistant, can be soldered or compression-fitted. More expensive and slower to install. Excellent for visible runs where aesthetics matter.

Plastic pipe (PEX/Hep2O)

Faster to install, flexible so fewer joints needed, doesn't corrode, cheaper. Not suitable for external or exposed to sunlight. Best for hidden runs under floors.

Which we recommend

For full re-pipes, plastic is faster and cheaper without sacrificing quality. For visible pipework or short sections, copper looks better and lasts just as long.

What Re-piping Involves

1

Survey the existing system

We map out where pipes run, identify all supply routes, and note where access is difficult. We plan the new routes to minimise disruption.

2

Isolate and drain

We turn off water, drain the system, and remove the old pipework section by section as we replace it.

3

Install new pipe

We run new pipe along the same routes or find better routes if access allows. We secure it properly and insulate where needed.

4

Connect and test

We connect to existing pipes at start and end points, test under full pressure, and check every joint for leaks before covering.

5

Restore and clean

We make good any floorboards or access holes we've opened, clean up, and show you where the new pipes run.

Common Questions

Got questions? Here's the quick answers.

How much does re-piping cost in Sheffield?
Single sections cost £150-£300 across Sheffield and surrounding areas. Full bathroom re-pipes cost £350-£600. Whole house re-piping for a 2-bed costs £1,200-£2,000. Supply pipes cost £400-£800.
How long does re-piping take?
Single sections take 3-5 hours. Bathroom re-pipes take 1-2 days. Whole house re-pipes take 2-4 days depending on size and access, whether you're in City Centre, Hillsborough, or Ecclesall.
Will you need to rip up my floors?
We lift floorboards to access pipes, but we do it carefully and reinstate them. If you've got fitted carpets, they'll need lifting temporarily. Solid floors are trickier, we route pipes differently to avoid breaking concrete.
Should I re-pipe the whole house or just the leaking section?
If one section has failed, others are likely close behind. Re-piping the lot in one job is more cost-effective than repeated call-outs. We'll advise honestly based on pipe condition.
Is plastic pipe as good as copper?
Yes, modern plastic pipe (PEX/Hep2O) is approved for all domestic plumbing. It doesn't corrode, lasts 50+ years, and is quicker to install. Copper is still excellent, just slower and more expensive.
Will re-piping improve my water pressure?
Yes, if low pressure was caused by corroded pipes restricting flow. New pipe has full bore diameter and no internal corrosion. Pressure returns to what your mains supplies.
Can you re-pipe while I'm living in the property?
Yes, we work room by room. Water is off only while we're connecting sections. You'll have water back on by evening each day. It's inconvenient but manageable.
Do I need to replace lead pipes?
Yes, lead pipes contaminate drinking water. If your supply pipe or internal pipes are lead, replace them. Modern regulations require it, and it's a health issue.
Will you guarantee re-piping work?
Yes, all pipework carries a 12-month guarantee on our workmanship. The pipes themselves have manufacturer warranties of 25-50 years depending on brand.
