Signs You've Got a Water Leak

⚠️

Damp or discoloured patches

Brown stains on ceilings or bubbling paint on walls usually mean water is getting where it shouldn't. The leak may be directly above, or it could be running along a joist from further away.

⚠️

Musty smell

That damp, earthy smell means moisture has been sitting somewhere long enough for mould to start. Even if you can't see it, it's there.

⚠️

Higher water bills

A hidden leak can waste 10,000+ litres a month. If your bill has crept up without any change in your usage, something's leaking.

⚠️

Boiler pressure keeps dropping

If you're topping up your boiler pressure every few days, there's a leak somewhere in the heating system. It might be under the floor or behind a wall.

⚠️

Sound of running water

With everything turned off, put your ear to the walls near pipes. If you hear a faint hiss or trickle, water is escaping.

How We Find and Fix Leaks

We don't just guess. Our engineers use a systematic approach to find exactly where the leak is:

1

Visual inspection

We check all accessible pipework, joints, valves, and connections first. Many leaks are visible once you know where to look, under sinks, around the boiler, at radiator valves.

2

Pressure testing

We isolate sections of your plumbing and heating and test each one for pressure loss. This tells us which circuit the leak is on without opening any walls.

3

Thermal imaging

For hidden leaks, our thermal cameras show temperature differences in walls and floors caused by escaping water. This pinpoints the leak location without any drilling.

4

Repair or replace

Once found, we fix it. That might mean tightening a joint, replacing a valve, cutting out a corroded section, or re-routing a pipe if the original route is inaccessible.

5

Test and confirm

We run the system under full pressure and monitor it to confirm the leak is completely fixed. No guesswork, we don't leave until it's sorted.

Common Causes of Water Leaks

Different types of leaks have different causes. Here's what we see most often in Sheffield homes:

Corroded pipe joints

Older compression fittings degrade over time, especially in hard water areas. The olive inside the fitting can crack or the pipe can wear thin where it sits inside the joint.

Failed push-fit connections

Push-fit plumbing is quick to install but the O-rings can perish, especially in hot water runs. We see a lot of these fail in kitchens and bathrooms.

Radiator valve leaks

The gland nut on a radiator valve can loosen over time, causing a slow drip. It's a 5-minute fix but left alone it'll damage your floor.

Washing machine and dishwasher connections

Hose connections work loose from vibration. A small drip behind a washing machine can go unnoticed for months until the floor rots.

Toilet cistern overflow

A faulty fill valve lets the cistern overfill. Water runs into the overflow pipe and either drips outside or runs internally down the wall.

Common Questions

Got questions? Here's the quick answers.

How much does water leak repair cost in Sheffield?
Visible, accessible leak repairs cost £89-£150 across Sheffield and surrounding areas. Hidden leaks behind walls or under floors cost £150-£350 including detection and repair. Leak detection surveys alone are £100-£180. We diagnose free if we do the repair.
How long does it take to find and fix a leak?
Finding a hidden leak takes 1-3 hours with thermal imaging and pressure testing. Once found, repairs take within 2 hours. If the leak is obvious and accessible, we can often fix it same-day in under an hour, whether you're in City Centre, West Bridgford, or Beeston.
My boiler pressure keeps dropping, is there a leak?
Yes, almost certainly. Boiler pressure drops when water escapes from the system, usually a small leak under floors, behind radiators, or from the boiler itself. We'll pressure-test sections to find where it's leaking and fix it.
Can you find leaks without damaging my home?
We use thermal imaging to spot temperature changes from escaping water, and acoustic listening to hear water flowing in pipes. These methods pinpoint leaks through walls without drilling. Only if necessary do we make small exploratory holes.
What if the leak is in a pipe inside the wall?
We use thermal cameras to locate it exactly, then make a small access hole to expose just that section. We repair the pipe and advise on patching the wall. We keep the disruption as minimal as possible.
How do I know if a leak is inside or outside my house?
Check your water meter with all taps off. If it's moving, water is escaping somewhere. Damp patches, mould, or wet spots in the garden suggest where. Underground supply pipes outside are your water company's responsibility up to your boundary.
Are small leaks worth fixing?
Yes. A pinhole leak wastes thousands of litres per month, raising your bill. It also causes hidden damage, mould, rot, structural issues, that costs far more to fix than the leak itself. Small leaks become big leaks.
