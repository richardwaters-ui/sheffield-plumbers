When temperatures drop below freezing, unprotected pipes in lofts, garages, and external walls freeze solid. The water inside expands as it turns to ice, putting massive pressure on the pipe walls. Most pipes don't burst while frozen, they crack from the pressure and only start leaking once they thaw and water flows again. In Sheffield's older properties around Ecclesall, Crookes, and Broomhill, unlagged pipes in roof spaces and along external walls are especially vulnerable.

We thaw frozen pipes safely using specialist heating equipment, never blowtorches or naked flames that risk bursting the pipe or starting fires. Our engineers work systematically from the tap back toward the stopcock, warming the pipe gradually. If a pipe has already cracked from the ice, we'll replace the damaged section immediately before it has a chance to flood your home.

Signs Your Pipes Are Frozen

⚠️

No water from taps

Turn on a tap and nothing comes out, or just a trickle. If it's only affecting certain taps (usually upstairs or in unheated rooms), those supply pipes are frozen.

⚠️

Frost on visible pipes

You can see frost or condensation on exposed pipes in the loft, garage, or under the sink. If you can see frost outside the pipe, the water inside is definitely frozen.

⚠️

Strange noises from pipes

Banging, clanking, or gurgling sounds when you run taps can mean ice is blocking part of the pipe, forcing water through a restricted gap.

⚠️

Boiler not firing

Some boilers shut down if the condensate pipe freezes. You'll see an error code, usually EA on Worcester boilers or F28 on Vaillants.

⚠️

Bulging or split pipe

The pipe looks deformed or you can see a visible crack. Don't touch it, the ice is holding back water that'll flood out when it thaws. Call us immediately.

What to Do If Your Pipes Freeze

1

Turn off the water at the stopcock

If you think a pipe might have cracked, shut off the main stopcock. This limits flooding when the pipe thaws. Leave the tap open to relieve pressure.

2

Warm the room gently

Open cupboard doors, turn on heating, use a portable heater in the affected room. Gradual warming is safer than trying to thaw the pipe directly.

3

Don't use direct heat

Never use a blowtorch, hairdryer, or heat gun on frozen pipes. The rapid temperature change can burst the pipe or, in the case of plastic pipes, melt them.

4

Check for leaks as it thaws

As the ice melts, watch for drips or sprays. If water starts leaking, turn off the stopcock immediately and call us.

5

Call us if you're uncertain

If you can't locate the frozen section, or the pipe is inaccessible, don't guess. We've got the equipment to find and thaw it safely without damage.

How We Thaw Frozen Pipes

Our engineers use specialist thawing equipment that's safe and controlled:

1

Locate the frozen section

We trace the pipe from the tap back toward the stopcock, feeling for the cold section. Infrared thermometers help us pinpoint exactly where the blockage is.

2

Apply gradual heat

We use electric pipe thawing mats, hot water towels, or radiant heaters, never naked flames. The pipe needs to warm slowly and evenly to avoid thermal shock.

3

Start from the tap end

We thaw from the open tap backward toward the frozen section. This lets water escape as the ice melts, reducing pressure on the pipe.

4

Monitor for cracks

As the pipe thaws, we check for splits, bulges, or weeping joints. If the ice has damaged the pipe, we replace that section before turning the water back on.

5

Test and lag vulnerable pipes

Once flowing, we run all taps to confirm the system's back to normal. We'll recommend pipe lagging or insulation for vulnerable sections to prevent future freezing.

Preventing Frozen Pipes in Sheffield

Sheffield gets cold enough most winters to freeze unprotected pipes. Here's how to protect yours:

Insulate exposed pipes

Foam pipe lagging costs pennies and works. Lag all pipes in lofts, garages, and along external walls. Pay special attention to north-facing walls that never see sun.

Keep heating on low

Set your heating to come on for an hour in the morning and evening, even when you're away. Keeping the house above 12°C stops pipes freezing.

Open cupboard doors

Pipes under sinks are in unheated cupboards. On very cold nights, open the doors to let warm room air circulate around the pipes.

Let taps drip overnight

Running water doesn't freeze as easily as still water. Let cold taps drip slowly on the coldest nights. Moving water helps prevent ice forming.

Insulate the loft properly

Don't insulate under the cold water tank, it needs rising heat from the house. Do insulate the tank itself and all pipework in the loft space.

Common Questions

Got questions? Here's the quick answers.

How much does frozen pipe repair cost in Sheffield?
Thawing accessible frozen pipes costs £95-£150 across Sheffield and surrounding areas. Pipes in lofts or concealed spaces cost £140-£220. If the pipe has burst from the ice, repair costs £180-£350 including cutting out the damaged section and fitting new pipe. Pipe lagging to prevent future freezing costs £8-£15 per metre.
How long does it take to thaw a frozen pipe?
Accessible pipes take 30 minutes to 2 hours to thaw safely. Pipes buried in walls or under floors take longer, 2-4 hours, because we need to warm them gradually without direct access. Rushing it risks bursting the pipe, whether you're in City Centre, West Bridgford, or Beeston.
Can I thaw frozen pipes myself?
Only if they're accessible and you can warm them gradually with towels soaked in hot water, working from the tap toward the frozen section. Never use a blowtorch, heat gun, or naked flame. If the pipe is in a wall or you're uncertain, call us.
Will frozen pipes always burst?
Not always, but it's a high risk. Water expands by about 9% when it freezes, which puts huge pressure on pipe walls. Modern plastic pipes handle it better than old copper, but both can crack. Most burst once they thaw and water pressure returns.
Why do pipes freeze in lofts more than downstairs?
Loft insulation keeps heat in your home, but that means the loft space itself stays cold. Pipes up there are exposed to freezing air with no rising heat to protect them. That's why loft pipes must be lagged separately.
What's the coldest it needs to be for pipes to freeze?
Pipes start freezing when temperatures drop below -3°C for several hours, especially overnight. Pipes in unheated spaces can freeze even when the outdoor temperature is above zero if there's no warmth reaching them.
Should I leave heating on when I go away in winter?
Yes, set it to 12-15°C and leave it on constantly. The cost is minimal compared to the damage from burst pipes. Also consider leaving cupboard doors open and draining the system if you'll be away for weeks.
My boiler is showing an error, could it be frozen?
Yes, condensate pipes freeze regularly. Worcester boilers show EA, Vaillants show F28 or F29. The white plastic pipe from the boiler to outside gets blocked with ice. We can thaw it and fit insulation to prevent it recurring.
Can frozen pipes damage my boiler?
Not directly, but if the condensate pipe freezes, the boiler shuts down for safety. This leaves you with no heating or hot water. Thawing the pipe gets the boiler running again, no damage done.
