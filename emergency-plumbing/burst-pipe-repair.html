Signs You've Got a Burst Pipe

⚠️

Sudden drop in water pressure

If your taps go from normal to a trickle, a pipe has likely burst somewhere in the system. Check all taps, if they're all weak, it's a mains pipe.

⚠️

Damp patches on walls or ceilings

Water stains that appear suddenly, especially after cold weather, are a classic sign. The pipe may have frozen overnight and cracked when it thawed.

⚠️

Sound of running water with nothing on

If you can hear water flowing when all taps are off, water is escaping somewhere. Turn off your stopcock immediately and call us.

⚠️

Unexplained spike in water bill

A slow leak from a cracked pipe can waste hundreds of litres a day without any visible signs. If your bill jumps, get it checked.

⚠️

Puddles or wet spots in the garden

Underground supply pipes can burst too. Wet patches in the garden that don't dry out, especially in a line, suggest a buried pipe has failed.

What to Do Before We Arrive

1

Turn off the stopcock

Your stopcock is usually under the kitchen sink or near the front door. Turn it clockwise to shut off the water supply. This is the single most important thing you can do to limit damage.

2

Switch off the heating

If the burst is on a heating pipe, turn off your boiler and central heating. This stops hot water circulating through the damaged section.

3

Open taps to drain the system

Once the stopcock is off, open the cold taps to drain remaining water from the pipes. This reduces pressure and limits further leaking.

4

Move valuables away from the water

Get electronics, documents, and anything water-sensitive away from the affected area. If water is coming through the ceiling, put buckets underneath.

5

Take photos for your insurance

Before you start cleaning up, photograph the damage. Your home insurance will need evidence. We can also provide a report for your claim.

How We Fix Burst Pipes

Our engineers carry everything needed for most burst pipe repairs on the van. Here's what happens when we arrive:

1

Isolate the damage

We confirm the stopcock is off and locate exactly where the burst is. Sometimes it's obvious, sometimes we need to trace the pipework or use detection equipment.

2

Assess the pipe condition

A burst can be a clean split, a pinhole leak, or a corroded section. We check the surrounding pipe to make sure we're not just patching a bigger problem.

3

Cut out and replace the damaged section

We remove the burst section and fit new copper or plastic pipe with compression or soldered joints. We don't do temporary fixes, it's done properly the first time.

4

Pressure test the repair

Before we leave, we turn the water back on and check every joint under full pressure. No drips, no weeps, it has to be perfect.

5

Clean up and advise

We clean up after ourselves. When we arrive, we'll explain what caused the burst and advise on preventing it in future - frost damage requires lagging vulnerable pipes to protect against winter freezes.

Common Causes of Burst Pipes in Nottingham

Nottingham gets cold enough in winter to freeze unlagged pipes, especially in older properties around Sherwood, Sneinton, and the Lace Market. Here are the most common causes we see:

Frozen pipes

Water expands when it freezes. Pipes in unheated spaces, lofts, garages, external walls, are most vulnerable. A frozen pipe doesn't always burst immediately; it often cracks and only leaks when it thaws.

Corrosion in older systems

Properties built before the 1970s often have original copper or even lead pipework. Over decades, the pipe walls thin from the inside out until they fail. If one section goes, others are likely close behind.

High water pressure

Nottingham's water pressure varies by area. Some properties in West Bridgford and Wollaton have higher-than-average pressure, which puts extra stress on joints and older pipes.

Poor previous repairs

We see this regularly, push-fit connectors that weren't seated properly, or compression joints that were overtightened. Previous bodge jobs fail under pressure, especially in cold weather.

Ground movement

Underground pipes can shift with soil movement, especially in clay-heavy areas around Carlton and Mapperley. This can crack joints or pull pipes apart at connections.
