Why You Need an Annual Service

⚠️

Boiler performance has dropped

It takes longer to heat the house or the hot water runs cold. This suggests scale buildup, a dirty burner, or a circulation problem. A service cleans and optimises it.

⚠️

Boiler is nearing the end of winter

After months of heavy use, components are worn. A spring service catches wear and tear before the next winter stresses the system.

⚠️

You've never had it serviced

If you've owned the house more than a year and haven't serviced the boiler, it needs attention. We'll check it's safe and efficient.

⚠️

It's been more than 12 months since the last service

Manufacturers recommend annual services. Beyond that, safety checks are overdue and efficiency drops.

⚠️

Insurance or warranty conditions

Some insurance and warranty policies require an annual service. A service record can support your claim if something goes wrong.

What Happens During a Service

1

We arrive and run the boiler through its cycle

We let it run normally and listen for any unusual noises or issues. This gives us a baseline.

2

We check the pilot light and ignition

We inspect the electrode and ensure the pilot lights reliably. A faulty electrode gets replaced.

3

We clean the burner and ventilation

Dust and scale accumulate over the year. We clean the burner head and check ventilation to the outside is clear.

4

We test gas tightness and pressure

Using a gas detector, we confirm there are no leaks at joints and seals. We check the system pressure is in the correct range.

5

We inspect pipes and connections

We look for signs of corrosion, leaks, or loose joints. Minor issues are tightened; significant damage is noted and we advise on repair.

The Service Process

Here's the full service checklist we work through:

1

Visual inspection of casing and surroundings

We check the boiler isn't damaged, water isn't pooling underneath, and ventilation to the outside is unobstructed.

2

Test the thermostat and controls

We confirm the thermostat switches the boiler on and off correctly, and the timer (if fitted) works.

3

Check the circulating pump

We listen and feel for vibration, a pump that's stuck or slowing down is less efficient and will fail soon.

4

Inspect the safety controls

The overheat thermostat, pressure relief valve, and flame supervision device are checked for operation.

5

Test the flue and ventilation

We check gases are exiting safely and not being drawn back into the room. A blocked flue is dangerous.

6

Provide a service certificate

We leave you with a certificate documenting what was checked, any faults found, and recommendations. Keep this for warranty or insurance.

Boiler Service Benefits in Sheffield

Regular servicing extends boiler life and saves money:

Prevents expensive breakdowns

We catch failing parts (pump, valve, electrode) during a service before they fail mid-winter. A £70 service beats a £300 emergency repair.

Maintains efficiency

Hard water scale buildup (common in Sheffield) reduces efficiency by 10-15% per year. Cleaning the burner keeps efficiency high and gas bills lower.

Maintains warranty validity

Many boiler warranties require annual servicing. Skip a service and you might void the warranty.

Improves safety

We confirm the boiler is burning gas correctly and ventilation is safe. Gas leaks or poor combustion are caught early.

Gives you peace of mind

Knowing your boiler has been professionally checked and is safe means you can use it with confidence all winter.

Common Questions

Got questions? Here's the quick answers.

How much does boiler servicing cost in Sheffield?
Annual boiler servicing costs £80-£120 depending on boiler type across Sheffield and surrounding areas. Combi boilers are usually £80-£100. System boilers with cylinders are £100-£120 due to additional checks. We check everything, clean key parts, and test for safety and efficiency.
How long does a boiler service take?
A full boiler service takes 45-90 minutes. We check gas pressure, clean burners and heat exchanger, test safety devices, inspect for leaks, and check efficiency. If we find minor faults, we'll fix them on the spot (small extra charge), whether you're in City Centre, West Bridgford, or Beeston.
Do I really need annual boiler servicing?
Yes. It keeps your boiler running efficiently (saving on bills), catches problems early before they become expensive repairs, and is required to keep manufacturer warranties valid. Most boiler breakdowns happen because servicing was skipped.
What does a boiler service include?
We check gas pressure and flow, inspect the burner and heat exchanger, test safety devices (pressure relief valve, flue), check seals and gaskets, test efficiency and emissions, inspect for leaks or corrosion, and test all controls. You get a service certificate afterward.
Can you service any boiler brand?
Yes, we're Gas Safe registered and service all major brands: Vaillant, Worcester Bosch, Ideal, Baxi, Viessmann, Potterton, and more. We follow manufacturer service schedules and use genuine parts where needed.
Will you fail my boiler if something's wrong?
Only if it's dangerous (gas leak, carbon monoxide risk, or safety device failure). For minor faults that aren't safety risks, we'll advise on repairs but won't condemn the boiler. Most issues found during servicing are fixable on the spot.
Can I get boiler servicing and plumbing repairs at the same time?
Yes, if you've got other plumbing jobs needed, we can combine visits. Tell us when you book and we'll allocate extra time. It's more efficient than two separate callouts, whether you're in City Centre, West Bridgford, or Beeston.
How often should I service my boiler?
Once a year, ideally in late summer before winter heating demand. Annual servicing is required by most boiler warranties. Skip it and your warranty is void. Regular servicing also prevents expensive breakdowns during winter.
