Signs Your Thermostat Has Failed

⚠️

Heating won't turn on

You've set the thermostat but the boiler doesn't fire. The thermostat isn't sending the demand signal. Could be dead batteries, wiring fault, or failed thermostat.

⚠️

Heating won't turn off

The house is roasting but the heating keeps running. The thermostat is stuck calling for heat or the relay has failed closed. Turn the boiler off at the mains until we arrive.

⚠️

Temperature wildly inaccurate

The thermostat shows 25°C but the house is freezing, or vice versa. The temperature sensor has failed. It can't regulate properly if it doesn't know the real temperature.

⚠️

Blank or flickering screen

Digital thermostats with blank screens usually have dead batteries or no power supply. If it's wired, check the fuse. If it still won't power up, the thermostat has failed.

⚠️

Lost wireless connection

Wireless thermostats that lose connection to the receiver stop controlling the heating. The receiver light flashes, the heating ignores the thermostat. Re-pairing fixes some, hardware failure needs replacement.

⚠️

Buttons not responding

You press buttons but nothing happens. The touchscreen or button membrane has failed. These can't be repaired, the thermostat needs replacing.

Common Thermostat Problems

Most thermostat issues have simple causes, but some need replacement:

Dead batteries

Wireless and battery-powered thermostats go blank when batteries die. Replace with good quality alkaline batteries. Cheap batteries from pound shops don't last.

Incorrect settings

After power cuts, programmers reset to factory settings. The schedule is wrong, the temperature setpoint is wrong. We reprogram it correctly.

Poor thermostat location

Thermostats in hallways near doors, above radiators, or in direct sunlight give false readings. Relocating the thermostat solves the problem permanently.

Wiring faults

Loose connections at the thermostat or boiler stop the demand signal getting through. We trace the wiring, test voltage, and fix any faults.

Failed internal components

The relay, sensor, or circuit board inside the thermostat can fail. These aren't user-repairable, the thermostat needs replacing.

How We Fix Thermostats

We diagnose systematically to find what's failed:

1

Check power and batteries

We test power supply to wired thermostats and replace batteries in wireless ones. Many apparent failures are just flat batteries.

2

Test the demand signal

We manually call for heat at the thermostat and check the signal reaches the boiler. If the boiler fires, the thermostat is working. If not, we trace the fault.

3

Check wiring continuity

We test wiring from thermostat to boiler with a multimeter. Loose terminals, broken wires, or blown fuses show up immediately.

4

Re-pair wireless systems

For wireless thermostats that have lost connection, we follow the manufacturer's pairing procedure. If pairing fails, the receiver or thermostat has failed.

5

Replace the thermostat

If the thermostat itself has failed, we fit a replacement. We carry common models and can source smart thermostats like Nest or Hive if you want to upgrade.

6

Calibrate and test

We set the temperature, wait for the boiler to fire, then check the heating cycles on and off correctly as the room reaches temperature.

Should You Upgrade to a Smart Thermostat?

If you're replacing a failed thermostat anyway, consider upgrading:

Remote control via app

Turn heating on from your phone before you get home. Handy if your schedule varies or you're away and forgot to turn the heating off.

Learning schedules

Nest and Hive learn your routine and adjust heating automatically. No more programming complex timers.

Energy reports

Smart thermostats show how much energy you're using and suggest savings. Typical savings are 10-15% on heating bills.

Cost

Smart thermostats cost £200-£250 fitted. Basic digital thermostats cost £120-£180 fitted. The extra £50-£100 for smart control pays back in 2-3 years from energy savings.

Common Questions

Got questions? Here's the quick answers.

How much does thermostat repair cost in Sheffield?
Diagnosis and simple fixes like reprogramming or battery replacement cost £75-£110 across Sheffield and surrounding areas. Basic thermostat replacement costs £120-£160. Wireless thermostats cost £140-£180 fitted. Smart thermostats like Nest or Hive cost £200-£280 fully installed.
How long does thermostat repair take?
Simple fixes take 15-30 minutes. Replacing a thermostat takes 30-60 minutes for basic models, within 2 hours for smart thermostats with app setup and testing, whether you're in City Centre, Hillsborough, or Ecclesall.
Can I replace a thermostat myself?
Simple like-for-like swaps are DIY-able if you're confident with electrics. But wiring mistakes can damage the boiler PCB (£200-£400 repair). For wireless or smart thermostats, professional installation ensures it's set up correctly.
Why is my thermostat screen blank?
Dead batteries if it's wireless. No power if it's wired, check the fuse in the boiler and at the thermostat. If it still won't power up after checking both, the thermostat has failed.
My heating runs constantly, what's wrong?
The thermostat is stuck calling for heat, or the relay has failed closed. Turn off the boiler at the mains to stop the heating. The thermostat needs replacing, or there's a wiring fault shorting the demand signal.
Are smart thermostats compatible with all boilers?
Most smart thermostats work with most boilers, but some older or more complex systems need specific wiring. We'll check compatibility during the assessment. OpenTherm boilers get extra features with compatible thermostats.
Can you move my thermostat to a better location?
Yes, we can relocate wired thermostats by extending wiring, or fit wireless thermostats that don't need wiring at all. Relocating to a central room away from drafts and direct heat improves temperature control.
Why does my thermostat show the wrong temperature?
It's in a bad location (near a draft, above a radiator, in direct sunlight), or the internal sensor has failed. Check where it's mounted first. If the location is fine, the sensor has failed and it needs replacing.
My wireless thermostat won't connect to the receiver, why?
Batteries might be low, or they've lost pairing. Try fresh batteries and re-pair following the manufacturer's instructions. If pairing fails repeatedly, either the thermostat or receiver has failed.
