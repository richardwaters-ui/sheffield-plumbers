Signs You Need a Drain Survey

⚠️

Blockages every few months

If the same drain keeps blocking, there's a structural problem. It might be roots, a collapsed section, or sagging pipe. Clearing it twice a year costs more than fixing it once.

⚠️

Persistent smell near the drain

A smell that doesn't go away after jetting suggests stagnant water, cracks allowing sewer air in, or a ruptured pipe leaking into the soil.

⚠️

Damp patches in the garden

Wet patches over where the drain runs, especially if they don't dry out, mean the pipe is broken and leaking. A CCTV survey will show exactly where.

⚠️

Multiple drains blocking at once

If the kitchen, bathroom, and toilet all block simultaneously, it's not separate problems, it's a blockage or collapse in the shared main drain. You need a survey to pinpoint it.

⚠️

Cracks or subsidence in the building

Ground movement can damage underground pipes. If your house has cracks, a survey will tell you if the drains are affected.

What to Do Before We Arrive

1

Clear access to the drain

Locate your drain access point (usually a manhole or gully). Clear away plants, paving, or debris so we can open it without delay.

2

Have someone home

We need to run water through while we're filming so the camera can navigate the pipe and show us flow. You'll usually flush the toilet or run a tap.

3

Mark the drain run on the plan

If you know where your drain runs (toward the main sewer, direction to the street), show us. It helps us navigate the camera and understand the layout.

4

Ask us to mark problem areas

If you know roughly where the recurring blockage is, point it out. We'll focus the camera there and show you what's happening.

How a CCTV Drain Survey Works

CCTV surveys are non-invasive and give us a complete picture. Here's what happens:

1

Access the drain

We open the manhole or gully and push the camera head into the pipe. It's attached to a flexible cable that we feed through the drain.

2

Navigate to the problem area

We control the camera from outside and guide it through the pipe, filming as we go. The footage appears on our screen in real time.

3

Document faults and defects

When we spot a problem, a crack, root, blockage, collapsed section, or joint failure, we note its location and take close-up footage.

4

Measure the pipe

We record the distance from the access point to each problem. This lets us mark the exact location on a plan so you know where the fault is.

5

Provide a report with photos and recommendations

You get a written report with still photos from the video, a map showing fault locations, and our advice on repair priorities.

Common Drain Problems We Find in Sheffield

Older properties in Sheffield, especially Victorian terraces in Sherwood and Sneinton, have clay pipes that degrade. Here's what surveys typically reveal:

Tree root intrusion

Roots push through cracked clay pipes, especially near gardens with trees or shrubs. They trap debris and cause blockages. Some roots can be jetted; severe cases need pipe repair or removal.

Cracked or broken pipes

Age, ground movement, or poor installation causes cracks. Water leaks out (wasting water and creating damp patches), and soil leaks in (blocking the pipe).

Sagging or collapsed pipes

Pipes subside from age or ground movement, creating low spots where waste and water pool. This is common in properties built on clay in areas like Carlton and Mapperley.

Misaligned or offset joints

Joints that don't line up properly trap debris and cause blockages. This often happens after ground movement or poor previous repairs.

Grease and sediment buildup

Even without structural damage, pipes accumulate grease, soap, and scale. A survey shows how serious it is and whether jetting or replacement is needed.

Common Questions

Got questions? Here's the quick answers.

How much does a CCTV drain survey cost in Sheffield?
Surveys up to 30m cost £150-£200 across Sheffield and surrounding areas. Longer surveys (30-50m) are £200-£250. Surveys with location mapping cost £220-£280. Many mortgage lenders require drain surveys before purchase, it's £150-£250 well spent to avoid £3,000+ repair bills later.
How long does a CCTV drain survey take?
Most surveys take within 2 hours depending on drain length and accessibility. We survey from the access point through to the main sewer if possible. Results are instant, we review findings with you on-site and provide a written report, whether you're in City Centre, West Bridgford, or Beeston.
Is a CCTV drain survey needed for house purchases?
Most mortgage lenders now require one as part of a full survey. It identifies defects like collapsed pipes, root intrusion, or cracks before you buy. Spending £150-£250 on a survey can save you from discovering a £3,000 drain repair bill after completion.
What does a CCTV drain survey show?
We send a camera through your drain and record any damage: cracks, collapses, tree root intrusion, blockages, grease buildup, misaligned joints, or sagging pipes. You get photos, video footage, and a detailed report showing problem areas and their exact location.
Can I do a CCTV drain survey myself?
You'd need specialist camera equipment, training to interpret what you're seeing, and knowledge of drain construction. DIY cameras are cheap but give poor-quality footage. Professional surveys are thorough, insurable, and acceptable for mortgage purposes.
What if the survey finds serious damage?
We'll show you exactly what's wrong and quote for repairs. Common issues like root intrusion or small cracks are often repairable without excavation. Collapsed sections need excavation and replacement. We'll prioritise what needs immediate attention vs what can wait.
Do I need a survey if my drain keeps blocking?
Yes. Recurring blockages mean an underlying problem, roots, cracks, sagging pipes, or severe buildup. A survey shows exactly what's causing it so we can fix it properly rather than just clearing the symptom every few months.
Can you survey drains under buildings?
Yes, we can survey drains under extensions, conservatories, or outbuildings. Access is from manholes or gullies outside. The camera snakes through the pipe regardless of what's built above. If there's no access, we might need to create one.
