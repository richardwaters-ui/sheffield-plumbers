A blocked toilet is one of those problems you can't ignore. If it won't flush, or worse, it's backing up and threatening to overflow, you need it sorted now. Most toilet blockages are caused by too much paper, wet wipes (even ones labelled flushable), or things that shouldn't be flushed at all. Older toilets with weak siphons are more prone to blocking, especially in Sheffield's Victorian properties around Sneinton, the Lace Market, and Radford.

We clear blocked toilets using specialist equipment: plungers, augers, and high-pressure water jetting for stubborn blockages. Most toilet blockages are in the toilet trap or the first bend of the waste pipe, so we can clear them without removing the toilet. If the blockage is further down the drain, we'll rod or jet the external drains to clear the whole system.

We operate across all Sheffield areas with emergency response available. No call-out fee, just the clearance cost. Most toilet blockages are cleared in 30 minutes to an hour.

Signs Your Toilet Is Blocked

⚠️

Water rises when you flush

You flush and the water level rises nearly to the rim before slowly draining away. This is a partial blockage in the trap or waste pipe. Next flush might overflow.

⚠️

Slow draining bowl

After flushing, the water takes much longer than normal to drain. The blockage is restricting flow but not completely blocking it yet. It'll get worse.

⚠️

Gurgling noises

Air trapped by the blockage causes bubbling and gurgling sounds when you flush or when other drains are used. This means the drainage system is restricted.

⚠️

Won't flush at all

You flush but nothing happens, the water just sits there. The blockage is complete. Don't keep trying to flush, you'll overflow the pan.

⚠️

Other drains affected

The shower or bath gurgles when you flush the toilet, or the toilet water level drops when you run a tap. This means the main drain is blocked, not just the toilet.

What to Do Before We Arrive

1

Stop flushing

Don't keep trying to flush, you'll overflow it. Once you know it's blocked, leave it alone until we arrive.

2

Put towels down

If it looks like it might overflow, put towels around the base to soak up any spill. Move bath mats and anything else that could get soaked.

3

Don't use other drains

If the main drain is blocked, using sinks or showers will back up into the toilet. Avoid using plumbing until we've cleared the blockage.

4

Locate your drain access

We might need to rod the external drains. Check where your manhole covers are, usually in the back garden. If they're buried or hidden, knowing roughly where helps us work faster.

How We Unblock Toilets

Our engineers carry the tools to clear most toilet blockages on site:

1

Assess the blockage location

We check if it's in the toilet trap (most common), the waste pipe, or further down the main drain. Other signs like gurgling sinks or multiple blocked drains tell us it's a main drain blockage.

2

Plunge or auger

For blockages in the trap, we use a heavy-duty plunger or closet auger to break up and dislodge the blockage. Most toilet blockages clear this way in 10-20 minutes.

3

Chemical-free approach

We don't use harsh chemicals that damage seals and pipes. Mechanical clearance works better and doesn't harm your plumbing.

4

Rod or jet the drains

If the blockage is in the waste pipe or main drain, we rod or jet from the outside access points. This clears the entire drain run, not just the immediate blockage.

5

Test and advise

We flush multiple times to confirm it's cleared completely. We'll advise on what caused it and how to prevent it happening again.

Common Causes of Toilet Blockages

Most toilet blockages are preventable. Here's what we find blocking toilets in Sheffield homes:

Wet wipes

Even ones marked flushable don't break down like toilet paper. They catch in the trap or waste pipe and build up. Never flush wipes, bin them.

Too much toilet paper

Older toilets with weak siphons can't handle large amounts of paper in one flush. If you've used a lot, flush halfway through.

Sanitary products

Tampons, pads, cotton buds, they don't break down and they catch in bends. Always bin them, never flush them.

Foreign objects

Kids flush toys, adults accidentally drop things in. Anything solid that fits through the trap can block the waste pipe. We've removed all sorts.

Old pipework

Victorian properties often have narrow cast iron or clay waste pipes that block more easily than modern plastic. If your toilet blocks repeatedly, the pipework might need upgrading.

Common Questions

Got questions? Here's the quick answers.

How much does toilet unblocking cost in Sheffield?
Simple blockages in the toilet trap cost £85-£120 across Sheffield and surrounding areas. Stubborn blockages requiring an auger cost £110-£150. If we need to rod the external drains, it's £95-£180. Combined toilet and drain clearance costs £140-£220.
How long does it take to unblock a toilet?
Simple blockages take 15-30 minutes. Stubborn blockages take 30-60 minutes. If we need to rod external drains, allow within 2 hours for the full clearance and testing, whether you're in City Centre, Hillsborough, or Ecclesall.
Can I unblock a toilet myself?
Try a plunger first, create a seal around the outlet and plunge firmly 15-20 times. If that doesn't work, don't use chemicals, they rarely work on toilets and can make things worse. Call us instead.
What if the toilet keeps blocking?
Recurring blockages mean either something's being flushed that shouldn't be, or there's a problem with the waste pipe (partial collapse, tree roots, poor gradient). We'll investigate with a drain camera if needed.
Will you need to remove my toilet?
Very rarely. Most blockages are in the trap or first bend, accessible from above with an auger. We only remove toilets if there's a completely jammed foreign object or the toilet itself needs replacing.
Are chemical drain unblockers safe for toilets?
No, avoid them. They rarely work on toilet blockages (the water in the trap dilutes them), and they can damage internal seals and the porcelain. Mechanical clearance is safer and more effective.
My toilet is overflowing, what do I do?
Turn off the isolation valve behind the toilet to stop water flowing into the cistern. Mop up the overflow. Don't flush again. Call us immediately, this is an emergency.
Can wet wipes really block drains?
Yes, massively. They don't break down like toilet paper, they bind together and catch on any rough spot in the drain. We pull out massive clumps of wipes from blocked drains regularly. Bin them, never flush them.
Why does my toilet block but others in the house don't?
That toilet's trap or waste pipe has a narrower bore, sharper bends, or weaker flush. Older toilets are more prone to blocking. If it's a persistent problem, consider upgrading to a modern close-coupled toilet with better flush power.
