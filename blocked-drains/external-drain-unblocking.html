When an outside drain blocks, water backs up into the gully, floods the garden, and sometimes flows back into the house through floor drains or toilets. External drains carry wastewater from kitchens, bathrooms, and surface water from roofs and patios. They block with leaves, mud, grease, wet wipes, and tree roots. In Sheffield's older properties around Sherwood, Nether Edge, and Crookes, clay drains are prone to root intrusion and collapse, especially near mature trees.

We clear external drains using high-pressure water jetting and manual rodding. Jetting blasts through grease, sludge, and roots. Rodding is better for solid blockages like compacted debris or collapsed pipes. It's a problem we see regularly across Sheffield, from Victorian properties in Sneinton to newer builds in Ecclesall. We locate the blockage using drain rods or CCTV, clear it completely, and flush the system to confirm it's flowing freely.

Most external blockages are cleared in within 2 hours.

Signs Your External Drains Are Blocked

⚠️

Gully overflowing

Water fills the outside gully and won't drain away. It pools around the drain cover and floods onto the patio or driveway. This is a complete blockage downstream.

⚠️

Slow draining outside drain

Water eventually drains but takes minutes instead of seconds. This is a partial blockage building up. It'll get worse and fully block soon.

⚠️

Toilets or sinks backing up

Internal drains gurgle or back up when you use other plumbing. This means the external drain is blocked and wastewater has nowhere to go.

⚠️

Bad smell from drains

Foul smell from outside gullies or manholes. Standing water in blocked drains goes stagnant and smells terrible. It means the drain isn't flowing.

⚠️

Manhole full of water

Lift a manhole cover and the chamber is full of water or sewage. There's a blockage between that chamber and the next one downstream, or at the connection to the main sewer.

Common Causes of External Blockages

External drains block for different reasons than internal pipes:

Leaves and debris

Autumn leaves, mud, and silt wash into gullies and build up in the pipes. Gullies without covers or with damaged gratings catch more debris.

Grease and fat

Kitchen waste fat solidifies in drains once it cools. It coats the pipe walls and traps debris, slowly building a blockage. Never pour fat down sinks or drains.

Tree roots

Roots grow into cracks in clay drains seeking water. They spread inside the pipe and catch debris, creating blockages. Older properties with mature trees suffer this regularly.

Collapsed or damaged pipe

Clay pipes crack with ground movement or age. The collapsed section restricts flow and catches debris. This needs repair, not just clearing.

Wet wipes and sanitary products

Things that shouldn't be flushed catch in external drains. Wet wipes bind together and form solid blockages. They don't break down like toilet paper.

How We Clear External Drains

We use the most effective method for each blockage:

1

Locate the blockage

We lift manhole covers to find which section is blocked. Water backing up tells us where the blockage is. If all chambers are clear, the blockage is at the connection to the main sewer.

2

Rod the drain

We feed flexible drain rods from the upstream chamber toward the blockage. We screw on different attachments: plungers, corkscrews, scrapers, depending on what we're clearing.

3

Jet the drain

For grease, sludge, and root blockages, high-pressure water jetting works better. The jet blasts through the blockage and scours the pipe clean. It's faster and more thorough than rodding.

4

Clear the gully

We remove leaves, sludge, and debris from the gully trap itself. Often the blockage is right there at the grating, not in the drain.

5

Test and flush

We run water down the drain and watch it flow through the manholes. We confirm the blockage is completely cleared and the system is flowing freely.

6

Advise on prevention

We'll tell you what caused it and how to prevent it recurring: fitting gully guards, clearing leaves regularly, or dealing with tree roots.

Preventing External Drain Blockages

1

Clear leaves regularly

In autumn, clear leaves from gullies and roof gutters weekly. Leaves are the main cause of external blockages.

2

Fit gully guards

Wire or plastic guards over gully gratings catch leaves before they enter the drain. Empty them regularly, don't let debris build up on top.

3

Never pour grease down drains

Let fat cool and bin it, don't pour it down the sink. Even outside drains, grease solidifies and causes blockages.

4

Deal with tree roots

If you've got mature trees near drains, have the drains jetted annually to clear roots before they cause a full blockage. Consider lining the pipe to stop roots getting in.

Common Questions

Got questions? Here's the quick answers.

How much does external drain unblocking cost in Sheffield?
Single gully clearance costs £85-£120 across Sheffield and surrounding areas. Drain rodding costs £95-£150. High-pressure jetting costs £120-£200. Clearing multiple gullies costs £140-£220. Most jobs take within 2 hours.
How long does external drain clearance take?
Simple gully blockages take 30 minutes to 1 hour. Drain rodding or jetting takes within 2 hours depending on the length of the run and severity of the blockage, whether you're in City Centre, Hillsborough, or Ecclesall.
Can I clear external drains myself?
You can try lifting the gully grating and removing visible leaves or debris. For blockages in the pipe itself, you'll need drain rods or jetting equipment. We'd recommend calling us for pipe blockages.
Why does my outside drain keep blocking?
Recurring blockages mean either tree roots are growing in, the pipe has collapsed and needs repair, or grease is building up from kitchen waste. We'll investigate with CCTV and recommend a permanent fix.
What if the blockage is on the water company's side?
We'll identify where the blockage is. If it's on your property, it's your responsibility. If it's in the main sewer beyond your boundary, that's Severn Trent's responsibility. We can help you report it.
Is jetting better than rodding?
Jetting is more thorough for grease, roots, and sludge. It cleans the pipe walls as well as clearing the blockage. Rodding works for solid blockages and is cheaper. We'll use whichever method suits your blockage.
Will clearing the drain damage my pipes?
No, rodding and jetting are safe for sound pipes. If the pipe is already cracked or collapsing, CCTV will show us before we attempt clearance. We won't force equipment through damaged pipes.
What are gully guards and do I need them?
Gully guards are wire or plastic covers that sit over the drain grating. They catch leaves and debris before they enter the drain. If you've got trees nearby or your gully blocks regularly, they're worth fitting.
Can you clear drains in the rain?
Yes, we work in all weather. Rain actually helps us see if the drain is flowing properly once cleared. Heavy rain can make excavation messier but doesn't stop us clearing drains.
